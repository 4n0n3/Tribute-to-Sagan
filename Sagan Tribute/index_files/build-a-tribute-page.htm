Carl E. Sagan

A man who inspired generations of scientists and engineers, myself included

An abridged timeline of the life of Carl Sagan:

  • 1934, November 9 - Emerges from the void in Brooklyn, NY
  • 1951 - Graduates from Rahway High School as valedictorian
  • 1952 - Enrolls at the University of Chicago
  • 1955 - Earns a Bachelor of Science degree in physics
  • 1956 - Earns a Master of Science degree in physics
  • 1958 - Gains top secret clearance with the U.S. Air Force and secret clearance with NASA while working on Project A119
  • 1960 - Earns a Doctor of Philosophy in astronmy and astrophysics with his doctoral thesis titled Physicsl Studies of the Planets
  • 1961 - Publishes an article in the journal Science on the atmoshpere of Venus while being a Miller Fellow at the University of California, Berkley
  • 1963 - Joins Harvard University as an assistant professor and begins working at the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory
  • 1968 - Joins Cornell University as an associate professor
  • 1970 - Becomes full professor of astronomy and directs the Laboratory for Planetary Studies
  • 1972 - Assembles the first physical message that was launched into space via a gold-plated plaque attached to the space probe Pioneer 10, the following year Pioneer 11 is launched carrying another copy of the plaque
  • 1977 - Refines his designs of his most elaborate message Voyager Golder Record, which was launched on the Voyager space probes
  • 1980 - Co-writes and narrates his award winning television series Cosmos: A Personal Voyage
  • 1981 - Receives “Humanist of the Year” Award
  • 1994 - Publishes A Pale Blue Dot: A Vision of the Human Future in Space
  • 1996, December 20 - Returns to the void after suffering from myelodysplasia for two years ultimately from pneumonia
